# Marge Briefing Policy

Defines how the morning briefing should feel and what it should contain.

## Tone

- warm, direct, pastoral
- never corporate
- never clinical
- concise enough to read quickly with morning coffee
- specific enough to act immediately

## Goal

The pastor should finish the briefing knowing:
- who needs care today
- what follow-up is overdue
- what small relational touches matter this week
- what message Marge can already draft for them

## Structure

Preferred order:
1. urgent care cases
2. visitor follow-up
3. absent members
4. prayer requests needing updates
5. birthdays and anniversaries
6. today's nudge

## Quantity guidance

Do not overwhelm.

Suggested display limits:
- urgent care cases: all active urgent items
- visitors: top 3–5
- absences: top 3–5
- prayer requests: top 3–5
- celebrations: top 5
- nudges: 1–2

Overflow should stay in the care queue.

## Action guidance

Every surfaced item should ideally support one of:
- draft a text
- mark contacted
- add note
- defer / snooze
- open full member detail
