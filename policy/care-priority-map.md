# Marge Care Priority Map

Defines how Marge ranks people and situations for the morning briefing and care queue.

## Priority Levels

### P1 — Urgent pastoral attention
Surface first, never bury.

Examples:
- death or immediate grief event
- hospitalization / emergency surgery
- crisis language in note or prayer request
- counseling situation marked high concern
- member or family in acute distress
- unresolved high-severity care case with no recent contact

**Default action:** top of briefing + active care queue + draft outreach ready

---

### P2 — Time-sensitive follow-up
Needs action soon, but not necessarily today before everything else.

Examples:
- first-time visitor within 24–72 hours
- prayer request with no update after threshold
- member absent past threshold and historically consistent
- recently disclosed job loss / health scare / family strain
- care case overdue for follow-up

**Default action:** briefing inclusion + draft text + due follow-up item

---

### P3 — Relational maintenance
Important for faithful pastoral care, but not urgent.

Examples:
- birthdays this week
- anniversaries this week
- low-severity nudges from prior notes
- check-ins on members not contacted in a long time
- lower-risk absence patterns

**Default action:** briefing inclusion when space allows

---

### P4 — Informational only
Logged, searchable, but not briefing-worthy unless context changes.

Examples:
- routine administrative notes
- completed follow-up items
- resolved prayer updates
- old visitor records with no active follow-up

**Default action:** retain in history, do not surface by default

---

## Tie-break rules

When two items share priority, rank by:
1. severity of pastoral need
2. time since last contact
3. recency of signal
4. likelihood the person will slip through the cracks
5. whether the pastor explicitly said to follow up

---

## Morning briefing guidance

Briefing should feel focused, not overwhelming.

Preferred ordering:
1. urgent care cases
2. visitor follow-up
3. absent members requiring attention
4. prayer requests needing update
5. birthdays and anniversaries
6. one or two nudges

If too many items qualify, Marge should:
- prioritize P1 and P2
- summarize overflow rather than dump everything
- preserve the rest in the care queue
