# Research

## Competitors / alternatives
- generic CRMs used by churches
- church management systems with weak pastoral workflow support
- manual combinations of Rock + staff memory + spreadsheets + email drafts
- AI note/chat tools without ministry-specific workflow design

## Insights
- Marge is strongest when it acts like a daily pastoral workflow assistant, not a generic chatbot
- churches are likely to buy clarity, follow-through, and saved pastoral time more than AI novelty
- trust and privacy posture matter heavily in any church-care workflow product

## Open questions
- what exact pilot packaging produces the fastest close?
- what proof points most reduce pastor skepticism?
- how much setup friction will churches tolerate before pilot conversion drops?

## Opportunities
- position Marge as the practical ministry-ops assistant for daily care follow-through
- sell pilot value through specific workflows instead of broad AI language
- create tighter onboarding and runbook artifacts that reduce sales friction
